# Archive Workbench

## Purpose

Archive Workbench is a curation environment designed to bring together digital assets, research notes, metadata, artificial intelligence, and publication into a single coherent workflow. It is not another cataloging system but serves as the curator's workspace where archival materials are verified, enriched, documented, and prepared before publication.

## Vision

Archives often require information from many independent systems. Digital asset managers, research notebooks, spreadsheets, and JSON exports all house different parts of an archive. Public access may be provided through systems such as Omeka S. Archive Workbench aims to bridge the gaps between these systems, coordinating them rather than replacing them.

## Mission Statement

Provide historians, genealogists, archivists, museums, and cultural heritage organizations with a workflow that transforms scattered research and digital assets into verified, publishable archival records.

## Guiding Principles

- **Human First**: The human curator is always the authority. Automation exists to reduce repetitive work but should never obscure important decisions. Every AI-generated description requires human approval.
- **Archive First**: Version 1 is optimized for the Haushalter-Casler Family Archive. Generalization is encouraged only when it naturally emerges from solving real archival problems. Premature abstraction is avoided.

## Scope

Archive Workbench is responsible for:
- Importing metadata
- Importing digital assets
- Importing research
- Validating records
- Assisting historical description writing
- Preparing records for publication
- Uploading approved records to Omeka S
- Tracking workflow progress

Archive Workbench is not responsible for:
- Long-term digital preservation
- Image editing
- Transcription software
- Digital asset management
- Replacing Omeka S
- Replacing Obsidian
- Replacing DigiKam

## Primary Workflow

1. Create Project
2. Import Sources
   - Images
   - Documents
   - Metadata
   - Research Notes
3. Validate Records: Ensure that all imported records are accurate and complete.
4. Assist Historical Description Writing: Use AI to help write historical descriptions.
5. Prepare Records for Publication: Ensure all records are ready for publication.
6. Upload Approved Records to Omeka S: Publish the collection to Omeka S.
7. Track Workflow Progress: Monitor the progress of each task.
