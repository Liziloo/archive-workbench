## Collection Overview

**Date range:** 1866–2021 (Bulk dates: 1906–1999)

**Finding Aid Created By:** Elizabeth Casler Loomans, 2025

**Archive Created By:** June Haushalter Casler

**Extent:** 1 Archival File Box, 3 Archival Photo Boxes, 3 Storage Bins, 1 Apple Box, Oversized loose volumes, and 7 Standalone Material Artifacts / Family Heirloom Volumes.

**Abstract:** The Haushalter and Casler family records include photographs, correspondence, legal documents, military service files, regional histories, and ephemera documenting life on the 50-acre family farm in Hamburg, NY, the local history of Water Valley, and subsequent generations across the 20th century.

**Repository:** The Haushalter-Casler Family Archive, Private Collection (Cincinnati, OH)

**Access Restrictions:** The physical collection is housed in a private repository. Access is available to qualified researchers and academic affiliates by prior appointment only.

**Copy & Consultation Note:** Digital surrogates of processed materials can be provided upon request. For consultations regarding un-digitized physical items, arrangements may be made for on-site review or supervised access at a mutually agreed-upon local public venue (such as a library or local meeting space) following credential verification. Please use the site contact form to submit a research proposal.

**Language:** The material is primarily in English, with select historical volumes and certificates in German.

**Acquisition Information:** The Haushalter–Casler Family Papers comprise a multi-accession family archive unified by continuous family custody and a place-based narrative anchored in a single farm property held by interrelated families for over 150 years.

- **Accession 1**: Consists primarily of photographic materials (circa 1880–early 2000s) documenting family life and farm landscapes, with supporting documents, organized by the donor following the death of June Haushalter Casler.

- **Accession 2**: Forms the documentary core of the collection, adding original land, estate, military, and community records assembled by June Haushalter Casler (including the 2026 integration of the John Casler papers and digital photo proxies from Carl Haushalter's estate records).

- **Accession 3** (Anticipated): Consists of approximately twenty-five (25) handwritten World War II letters authored by Glenn Haushalter, providing an intimate first-person narrative closely tied to existing materials.

## Historical Note

The Haushalter family lived and worked on the same land in Hamburg, New York for 152 years. The original 50 acres was sold to Jacob Haushalter in 1856 by his father-in-law, Daniel Schweikhart. In 2008, June Haushalter Casler sold the final remaining acres of the homestead following the death of her husband, Edwin Casler. These records were collected by June over the course of many years, with the bulk of the historical contents handed down to her by prior generations, alongside later documents and photographs relating to the Casler family after her marriage to Edwin.

## Scope and Content Note

The collection consists of photographs, legal and personal documents, volumes, ephemera, and photo negatives. Following the 2026 accession from the John Casler papers, the collection has been re-processed into a unified structure focusing strictly on the lineage, the homestead, and family military service.

The collection is organized into five distinct series based on format, theme, and content:

- **Series I: Documents and Ephemera:** Correspondence, personal written recollections, legal papers regarding the farm, newspaper clippings, and commemorative ribbons.

- Subseries A: Core Household & Property Records

- Subseries B: Water Valley Regional History

- Subseries C: 20th-Century Military & Wartime Service

- Subseries D: John Casler Career History


- **Series II: Books and Volumes:** Family record books, regional history texts, personal religious texts, and celebration guest books.

- **Series III: Photographs:** Loose and cardboard-backed prints spanning circa 1866 to 2003, including farm views, family portraits, and digital scan proxies of Carl Haushalter's personal collection related to his career.

- **Series IV: Photo Negatives:** A small collection of mid-twentieth-century negatives.

- **Series V: Realia and Artifacts:** Standalone family heirlooms, fine art marquetry, and material culture implements associated with the family lineage.

## Unprocessed Materials

### Bin 4
This container primarily holds modern family, educational, and professional records, alongside personal ephemera belonging mostly to Liz and John Casler. Its contents range from childhood mementos, school photos, and college transcripts to career documents, legal paperwork, travel guides, and household correspondence. Among these personal items, a few objects hold specific historical interest, including an unused Kennedy-Johnson presidential campaign bumper sticker, transcribed notes from a conversation with Jack Casler reflecting on his World War II experiences, and official government or legal papers such as sealed Bar admission documents and a confidential Bath Ironworks memorandum.

### Bin 11
Serving as a substantial genealogical and regional archive, this container is filled with vital family records, legal deeds, correspondence, and extensive local history documentation regarding Water Valley and Eighteen Mile Creek for the Haushalter, Casler, Stunick, and Thomas families. Notable items of key historical significance include World War I ephemera, war bonds, and correspondence belonging to Anna and Minnie Stunick; detailed World War II military records, ration books, and service notes for multiple family members; an FDR recognition certificate for Glenn Haushalter; a December 30, 1939 front-page issue of the _Buffalo Evening News_ featuring Adolf Hitler; a full January 13, 1944 issue of the _Erie County Independent_; and historical clippings detailing a local Water Valley manufacturer that produced wooden presentation boxes for Purple Hearts.

### Apple Box
This container serves as a compact collection dedicated strictly to mid-20th-century historical print media and journalism. Its contents consist of original Buffalo-area newspaper issues bearing major front-page headlines from critical historical moments—including World War II, the Vietnam War, and the Space Race—as well as a curated selection of _Time Magazine_ issues published during World War II.

### Carl’s Bin
This container functions mainly as a visual and photographic archive, containing family photo albums, loose vacation snapshots spanning several decades, travel brochures, and personal mementos associated with Carl Haushalter and his relatives. Specific items of notable historical value stored within the bin include original Air Force base photographs, a copy of the July 21, 1969 _New York Times_ front-page cover story detailing the Apollo 11 moonwalk, and a formal 2003 Stage 1 Cultural Resource Investigation report conducted for the family farm.
